Dual context layers

Knowledge in brain-stem lives at two layers. You do research inside a project, where it starts as rough notes and settles into finished findings. When a finding is general enough to help any project, it can be promoted up to the universal Library — a curated shelf every future project inherits. This page is how the two layers relate, and how research climbs from one to the other instead of being redone.

Project research is rolled up under Projects/; the universal Library is surfaced as Wiki/. They are deliberately different assets — this page keeps them straight.

Research is born in a project and earns its way up. The scrub is the gate: strip the one project's specifics and what's left is reusable everywhere — or there was nothing general to keep.

Layer one: research lives in a project first

You don't research in the abstract — you research to move a project forward. So research starts at the project layer, in two grades:

  • Raw research — the Workshop. A scratch bench for in-progress work, with no required format and no rules, because forcing structure onto scratch paper only slows the thinking. It's ephemeral and never loaded into a session automatically, and it has no vault view on purpose. It exists to be thought in, and is read at exactly one moment — when a piece is promoted out of it.
  • Finished research — the project's record. When a piece settles, it's filed as durable project research in the plan that produced it (declared in that plan's manifest), and the project binder rolls every plan's findings into one generated research index. This is the project's accumulated knowledge — read on demand, inherited by the project's next session.

That is the whole first layer: rough notes become finished findings, and the binder gathers them so nothing a project learned is lost between sessions. Most findings stop here — they're true for this project and don't need to go anywhere else. Research is only one of the things a binder holds — alongside the decision log, the handoff chronicle, and the hand-maintained cover; the full anatomy and how a project is identified are on The project binder.

Layer two: the universal Library

Some findings, though, are general — true for any project, not just the one that produced them. Those can rise to the Library: the universal shelf of reusable reference, surfaced as Wiki/. It's a different asset from a project's research, and it's built to stay trustworthy:

  • The curated article. One article, one concept — a short type: reference file in a topic folder, written to be reused. Every topic folder and the Library root carries an _index.md, so the shelf is always labeled.
  • The immutable raw source. The project material an article was distilled from is kept unchanged in a _raw/ sub-folder. The polished article can be rewritten freely; the record of where it came from never is.
  • One change log. A single library-wide log.md records one line per event — added, updated, superseded — so there's one place to see what changed and when.
Each layer keeps a raw-and-finished pair — for different reasons

At the project layer, "raw" is the Workshop scratch (ephemeral, thrown away) and "finished" is the project's filed research. At the universal layer, "raw" is the immutable _raw/ source (kept forever) and "finished" is the curated article. The project keeps raw to think; the Library keeps raw to stay accountable. The Library is not loaded into every session — a full shelf every time would be enormous and mostly irrelevant; it loads only at the moment of need (below).

Promotion — from project research to a universal asset

Promotion is the bridge between the two layers, and it's a transformation, not a copy. It is something you ask for: you mark a finished piece of project research as universal by declaring it finalized in the plan's manifest. That declaration is the selectivity gate — nothing promotes just because it's sitting on the Workshop bench.

Propose first, then apply — never silently

Promotion runs in two passes, because a scrub that strips too much — or too little — needs a human backstop. A propose pass scrubs the candidate and writes nothing; it shows you the diff of what would be stripped. Only an explicit apply writes the article. It is deliberately not automatic.

On apply, the routine:

  1. Scrub. Strip everything specific to the one project that produced the draft — names, dates, one-off details — leaving the general, reusable truth. This is the gate: a draft that is nothing but project-specifics has nothing left, and never becomes an article.
  2. Preserve the source. The original, unscrubbed project material is copied unchanged into the new article's _raw/ — so the universal asset always remembers exactly what it grew from.
  3. Shelve and index. The scrubbed article is written into its topic folder and the change is recorded in the library log; the topic and root indexes are re-derived separately by the librarian.

The plain version: the project is where you think; the Library is what you keep.

Where the assets live — the file map

Concretely, a research asset moves through three homes (with a scratch pre-stage), each a real place on disk. The whole tree lives under ~/.claude-plans/, mirrored into your notes app by symlink:

The two concrete structures — project side, then universal side:

~/.claude-plans/
├── <plan>/                         a plan, in the plans folder
│   ├── manifest.json                declares research_artifacts[] (finalized = promotable)
│   └── _research/                   finished project research lives here  ← the source of truth
│       └── <artifact>.md
└── _projects/<project>/             the project binder            → surfaced as Projects/<project>/
    ├── _situating.md                the status card (generated; loaded each session)
    ├── hub.md                       the cover (curated; read on demand)
    ├── research-index.md            rolls up every plan's research (generated)
    └── research/<plan>/  →          symlinks back to each plan's _research/
~/.claude-plans/_library/            the Universal Store          → surfaced as Wiki/
├── _index.md                       root index of every topic
├── log.md                          one line per event: added · updated · superseded
└── <topic>/
    ├── _index.md                   topic index
    ├── <article>.md                the scrubbed reference article (type: reference)
    └── _raw/                       immutable copy of the unscrubbed project source

The plan's _research/ is the source of truth; the binder indexes it (it doesn't relocate it); promotion writes a separate, scrubbed copy into the Library and keeps the original beside it in _raw/. Project assets stay under Projects/; the one universal asset lands under Wiki/.

Before new research: check the Library first

The two layers close the loop at the one moment it matters — the start of new research. When a request looks like research, a small message-time check consults the Library index and surfaces a compact coverage pointer: there may already be reference on this, and here's how to read it. It is advisory — it never blocks, and it's silent when the Library is empty. Its job is one thing: reuse what's already universal before you redo it.

The check reuses; promotion contributes

The check only leverages existing reference — it does not scan for gaps to fill. Enriching the shelf happens the other way, through promotion (above), which you trigger when a finished finding generalizes. So when the check is silent on a topic, that absence is simply your cue that there may be something worth promoting up once your research is done. Together — reuse on the way in, contribute on the way out — universal knowledge flows from one project into the next without anyone maintaining a cross-project index by hand.

How a project is identified

All of this depends on one question — which project am I in? — answered the simplest way: a project is the directory you launched from. Both project-scoped surfaces (this project's memory and its research/binder) key off it, every session, with no list to keep in sync. (See What loads each session.)

Why this design

ChoiceThe reasoning
Research starts at the project layerYou research to move a project; forcing every note to be "universal-ready" up front would tax the thinking. Generality is recognized later, not demanded early.
The universal Library is a separate assetMixing one project's specifics into a shared shelf makes the shelf untrustworthy. A distinct, scrubbed, indexed asset stays reference-grade — which is exactly why the title keeps the two layers apart.
Promotion is declared, and propose-then-applyThe declaration keeps the shelf selective; the propose/apply split gives a human a chance to catch a bad scrub before it's written. Neither is automatic — on purpose.
Keep the immutable raw sourceA polished article you can rewrite is only trustworthy if the thing it was distilled from is preserved unchanged.
Check the Library at the start of researchA standing "remember to reuse" rule rots, and an after-the-fact check fires too late. A prompt-time check leverages existing reference at the only moment it can prevent a duplicate.

Reference

  • Raw project research (the Workshop) — ~/.local/state/brain-stem/workshop/: an ephemeral scratch bench; never loaded or indexed; no vault view; read only at promotion.
  • Finished project research — a plan's _research/ (declared in the manifest's research_artifacts[], finalized = promotable), rolled into the binder's generated research-index.md under ~/.claude-plans/_projects/<project>/ → Projects/.
  • The universal Library — ~/.claude-plans/_library/ → Wiki/: topic folders of one-concept type: reference articles; _index.md per topic and at the root; immutable _raw/ sources; one library-wide log.md.
  • The promotion (scrub) routine — user-declared (manifest finalized); propose (dry-run diff, writes nothing) then apply (scrub → preserve source in _raw/ → shelve + log; indexes re-derived separately by the librarian). Not automatic.
  • The moment-of-need Library check — a message-time background check that detects research intent and surfaces a compact coverage pointer; advisory, never blocks, silent when empty; leverages existing reference only (it does not flag gaps — enrichment is via promotion).
