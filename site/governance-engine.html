The engine · how every write is checked

The governance engine

The governance engine is a rulebook consulted on every file write. It is the heart of write-time governance — making sure each file, the moment it's created, follows the conventions that keep it discoverable. The discipline is structural rather than a matter of remembering to be careful: the rules are applied by machinery at the moment of the write, not left to anyone's good intentions.

One interception point (PreToolUse), three verdicts: allow · advise · deny.

Allow, advise, deny

Claude Code offers a single place to intercept a write (the PreToolUse hook) and natively only two answers: allow or deny. brain-stem adds the rulebook and a crucial third verdict — advise.

VerdictWhat happensUsed for
AllowThe write proceeds, silently.A well-formed write that needs no comment.
AdviseThe write proceeds, and a reminder is injected back to the assistant.The workhorse — most policy is taught, not blocked (a missing tag, a doc whose paired doc should update too).

The doorman runs on every write — but only acts inside the vault, and mostly advises rather than blocks.

Structural, not disciplinary

The point is that the check fires deterministically, every time, regardless of whether the assistant remembers the rule. Advise carries the day-to-day load; deny is the narrow guardrail for writes that would actually corrupt the structure.

Eight pillars

The rulebook is organized into eight pillars, each owning one slice of policy. They fall into three families by what they govern — a scope ladder from inside a single file outward: the single document (its labels and its body), across documents & folders (structure spanning files), and writers & lifecycles (operation rather than shape). Read each panel as what it keeps true, plus the slice it governs, a concrete example, and the verdict it leans on — most pillars teach (advise); a few guard structure and will block (deny).

How the rulebook is built

The eight pillars aren't eight files you wire together: they're composed at release time into one sealed, generated bundle (foundation-master.json) that you never edit by hand. How that composition works, and how you extend or override it through the overlay, is its own topic.

Extending or overriding the rulebook

The shipped rulebook is sealed, but you extend it — your own document types, folder rules, writers, and whole archetypes — through a separate, audited overlay seam, and you can override a shipped rule when it doesn't fit (with a stated reason on the record). That is its own topic: Governance personalization covers the overlay, the composition, and /govern register in full.

Where the guard acts — and where it can't

The guard's document-governance branches are VAULT_ROOT-gated: they only fire on a write whose path is inside the vault. This is deliberate — it keeps the engine from firing spurious advisories on every file on disk. (The plan-lifecycle branch is the main exception — it governs the plans tree at its own root, ~/.claude-plans/, not the vault.)

The Work area is governed at audit time

Because the gate is the vault root, writes to the Work area (which lives outside the vault) are never write-time governed. The work product you author there is covered instead by an audit-time backstop — a session-close parity check — which also catches cross-file drift that a single-write guard structurally cannot see. The one wrinkle: the system keeps a couple of navigation files in the Work area current for you (a what-lives-where map and folder indexes), but it never machine-writes your actual deliverables.

Fail-open by design

If the rulebook is missing, the engine allows the write. A corrupt overlay falls back to the foundation-only view. Missing machine identity exits quietly. The engine degrades toward letting you work, never toward locking you out.

Why this design

ChoiceThe reasoning
Enforce at write-time via a hookA standing written rule degrades over sessions; a hook fires regardless of the assistant's cooperation.
Advise as the default verdictMost policy should be taught, not blocked — blocking is reserved for structural corruption.
Compose pillars into a sealed, generated masterOne reproducible bundle to ship and reason about; edits live in the source pillars (see Governance personalization).
Gate on the vault root; audit outside itAvoids spurious advisories everywhere; the Work area and code repos are covered by audit, not write-time blocking.
