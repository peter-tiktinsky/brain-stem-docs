The writer and ingestion pipeline

The vault is a shared folder with two authors: a person editing by hand, and scripts writing on their own. Left uncoordinated, that combination drifts — two writes race and clobber each other, machine logs pile up where human notes belong, a regeneration overwrites a hand-edit. The writer pipeline is the small set of programs that prevent those failure modes. The design is reached on both grounds: the platform ships no shared-write coordinator (forcing the single-writer funnel), and three unrelated fields — content-addressable storage, event-driven systems, and knowledge management — each landed on the same structural answers independently.

The pipeline's default posture is deterministic: mechanical direct-write, no model in the loop. The LLM reasoning lane is an opt-in addition for the judgment calls that determinism cannot handle — not the default path, and not the writer. That distinction runs through every row below.
