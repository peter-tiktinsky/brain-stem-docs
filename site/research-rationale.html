The two kinds of "optimal"

brain-stem makes one promise about its own design: a choice is justified only if it is forced by the platform, or independently rediscovered by unrelated fields — never reached by taste. Holding that distinction is what makes the design auditable: you can check which kind of "optimal" a given choice rests on, rather than taking a designer's word for it.

Optimal by constraint

The platform forces the shape

Claude Code behaves a fixed way and only one design works. The auto-loaded memory file is hard-capped with no overflow path CC memory, so a single small index plus fetch-on-demand detail is not "a good idea" — it is the only shape the platform permits.

Optimal by convergence

Unrelated fields rediscovered it

Separate disciplines arrived at the same structure on their own. The three-way split of memory matches a fifty-year consensus in memory science Tulving 1972 Cohen & Squire 1980; "warn before you block" matches what policy engines, linters, and rollout systems each reached independently OPA Gatekeeper.

The meta-rule

Constraint settles what must be true; convergence confirms what ought to be. The strongest signal is a choice reached on both grounds at once — and the design holds because both kinds of pressure point the same way. A choice that is neither is, by the system's own rule, arbitrary.

The friction inversion

One cross-cutting property surprises people, and it follows directly from the two-grounds test. You might expect the system to let you reshape the surface conveniences and lock down the deep machinery. It is exactly the reverse.

The more universal a pillar, the more brain-stem lets you bend it

The governance engine — the most universal piece — has the richest customization seam: your overlay can override any rule. Plans — the most opinionated — has the thinnest. The pieces you are most likely to want to reshape are the ones built to be reshaped, because they are the ones where reshaping is safe. Universal equals safe to bend. The seams are the product: you don't assemble a toolkit, you move into a furnished foundation and personalize it at known seams.

The six design principles

Because the same non-arbitrariness test is applied everywhere, the same handful of principles surface in every section. These six are a different axis from the six sections above — the sections are what gets governed; the principles are the recurring moves for how every section is governed. For each, the engine chose a specific shape over a specific alternative, for a specific reason.

1

Advise before you block

A doorman at every write returns allow / advise / deny — and advise is the workhorse. Most policy is taught, not enforced by blocking.

Chosen over a binary allow/deny, and a deny-first design — blocking a save over a missing tag is hostile and just trains people to switch the guard off.

Grounded in OPA Gatekeeper K8s Pod Security ESLint Saltzer & Schroeder

constraint convergence

2

Two surfaces for every rule

Each rule is kept twice: a machine-readable form the assistant applies at write-time, and a human-readable page a person reads to understand the reasoning.

Chosen over one rulebook everyone hand-edits — which makes every upgrade overwrite local work and blurs custom rules into shipped ones.

Grounded in Kustomize git config CSS cascade

constraint convergence

3

Propose, then confirm

Where the system changes something on your behalf, it drafts the change and lets you accept, edit, or skip — rather than acting silently.

Chosen over silent auto-writes — the installer ships a provably write-free preview; memory promotion never auto-writes; new rules go through a guided interview.

Grounded in setup-wizard UX rustup check/apply

convergence

4

Structural, not disciplinary

Guarantees — an index stays current, a hand-edit always wins, "verified" is real — are enforced by machinery, not by anyone remembering to be careful.

Chosen over convention and good intentions — indexes are regenerated not hand-tended, and "verified" is machine-stamped so "done" cannot be forged.

Grounded in prose-rule decay #33603 PMI/PMBOK

convergence

5

Fail open, surface the problem

If a piece of the system is itself misconfigured, the default is to let your work continue while surfacing the problem — never to lock you out.

Chosen over fail-closed — a layer that locks you out on its own failure is worse than no layer.

Grounded in the availability-over-security doctrine — fail-open is the documented choice "where availability is more critical than security" AuthZed. This is a deliberate inversion of Saltzer & Schroeder fail-safe-defaults: a formatting/governance hook is outside the security-critical domain where fail-closed is mandated.

convergence

6

Your edits are sacred

Installing, re-running setup, and uninstalling all treat anything you personalized as something to preserve, never a side effect to discard.

Chosen over last-writer-wins — a hand-edit always wins via a two-signal check; upgrades diff against per-release baselines; uninstall preserves edited files.

Grounded in OT/CRDT survivorship chezmoi package-manager receipts

constraint convergence
